Alcohol, Medical Emergencies & the RAP
▼

UGA's Responsible Action Protocol (RAP) is one of the most important policies you need to know. If someone needs emergency medical help due to alcohol or drugs, call 911 or UGA Police immediately. The RAP means that the person who calls for help, and often the person who needed help, may not face mandatory alcohol/drug sanctions under the Code of Conduct.

Students under 21 are prohibited from possessing alcohol under UGA's Code of Conduct (Regulations 4.1–4.5). First offense minimum sanctions include an AOD education program and 6 months probation. Never hesitate to call for help — the RAP exists for exactly this reason.

All chapter social events with alcohol must be registered with the Greek Life Office in advance. Unregistered events with alcohol expose chapter leadership to conduct proceedings.

🗣️
Bias, Discrimination & Your Protections
▼

UGA's Non-Discrimination and Anti-Harassment Policy protects all students from discrimination based on race, color, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity, national origin, disability, age, or religion. This applies to Greek organizations and their members.

Title VI of the Civil Rights Act protects you from race-based discrimination in university programs. Title IX prohibits sex-based discrimination. If you experience identity-based harm from a Greek member, you can file a Bias Report at bias.uga.edu — this can be done anonymously.

Retaliation against anyone who reports a bias incident is itself a Code of Conduct violation. You are protected for reporting in good faith.

🚫
Hazing — What It Is & What To Do
▼

Under Georgia's Max Gruver Act (O.C.G.A. § 16-5-61), hazing is illegal. It is defined as any activity that endangers a student's physical health, or that coerces a student through social or physical pressure to consume food, liquid, alcohol, or drugs. Hazing is a misdemeanor of a high and aggravated nature — it is a crime, not a tradition.

"I consented" is not a defense under Georgia law. Willingness to participate does not make hazing legal.

If you are being hazed or witness hazing, you can report to the Office of Student Conduct at 706-542-1131. Reports can be made anonymously. UGA is required by law to publicly disclose all hazing adjudications within 15 days.

🛡️
Sexual Misconduct & Title IX
▼

Title IX guarantees that no student can be subjected to sex-based discrimination in any federally funded education program. This includes sexual harassment, assault, stalking, and dating violence involving Greek members on or off campus.

UGA has a Title IX Office and an Office of Victim Services to support you. Reporting to the Title IX Office triggers a formal institutional response. You can also seek confidential support without triggering an investigation by contacting a confidential resource like UGA Counseling (706-542-2273).

Greek chapter leaders and advisors who become aware of sexual misconduct involving members are mandatory reporters under UGA policy — they are required to notify the Title IX Office.

💰
Funding & the Public Money Restriction
▼

As a public university in Georgia, UGA is subject to state regulations that restrict the use of student activity fees for certain types of programming, including diversity, equity, and inclusion education.

This means that if your council wants to bring in a speaker or run programming on identity, inclusion, or bias prevention, that programming cannot be funded with student government money. Instead, use chapter operating funds, national HQ education grants, or private philanthropy budgets.

This is not a ban on the programming itself — it is a funding restriction. The GLO can help you find compliant funding pathways.

📊
UGA Standards of Excellence — What's Required
▼

Every chapter must meet UGA's Standards of Excellence (SOE) requirements to remain in good standing. Key requirements include: minimum 2.7 chapter GPA each semester, minimum 5 service hours per member per semester, all social events with alcohol must be registered with the GLO, and new members must complete Sexual Misconduct Prevention training within their first 2 weeks.

Chapters that fall out of good standing can face temporary suspension of privileges, loss of university recognition, or mandatory coaching programs.
